## What Is Crash Game?

Crash Game is a multiplier-based game where each round starts at **1.00x** and climbs visually until a fixed point — then it falls, or "crashes." Before the crash happens, you can press cash out to lock in winnings based on the multiplier at that moment.

Simple example: You bet 10,000 demo coins. The multiplier rises to **2.50x** and you cash out in time. Payout = 10,000 × 2.5 = 25,000 coins. If the crash happens before you cash out, your entire bet is lost for that round.

What you need to understand from the start: the chart you see is not a prediction of the future — it is only a visualization of a number **already determined** before the round begins. This system is called [Provably Fair](/blog/apa-itu-provably-fair), and understanding it will change how you view every crash game session.

## How Multiplier and Crash Point Work

Every crash game round has a **crash point** — the multiplier value where the round ends. The crash point is generated cryptographically from a combination of server seed, client seed, and nonce. That means nobody can "rig" when the crash happens after bets are closed.

Crash point distribution tends to produce many rounds with low multipliers (1.00x–2.00x) and occasional rounds with very high multipliers (50x, 100x, or more). This is what makes crash game feel fair yet volatile at the same time.

### Reading the Chart Correctly

The chart rises at varying speeds — sometimes slowly, sometimes rapidly. Visual speed **does not** indicate crash probability. Many beginners misunderstand this: "The chart is rising fast, it must crash soon." In reality, the crash point was fixed from the start.

The only thing you can control is one decision: **when to cash out**. That is the core of crash game strategy — not guessing the chart, but managing risk and discipline.

## Basic Cash Out Strategy for Beginners

Crash game strategy is fundamentally a strategy of **when to exit**. There is no magic formula, but some approaches are more sustainable than blindly chasing high multipliers.

### Conservative Multiplier Targets (1.5x–2.2x)

Beginners are often tempted to chase 10x, 20x, or more after watching someone else's round. Crash point distribution statistics show that low multipliers appear far more often. Targets of **1.5x–2.2x** consistently deliver a higher win rate, even though payout per round is smaller.

Example: With a 2.00x target and a win rate around 45–50%, you need strong discipline — but session variance is much more controlled compared to chasing 5x with a 15–20% win rate.

### Ladder Strategy

Some players split their bet into two parts: half cashed out at 1.5x (securing capital), half left until 3x or 4x. This approach reduces the psychological pressure of "all or nothing" and works well for demo practice sessions.

### Adjusting Targets to Session Volatility

If you experience 5–7 consecutive losses at a 2x target, that is normal statistically — not a sign the system is "cold." What matters: do not raise your bet emotionally. Stick to the plan, or end the session if your mental state starts to waver.

## Martingale in Crash Game: Why It Is Risky

Martingale is a betting system where you **double your bet after each loss**, hoping one win will cover all previous losses plus a small profit.

Simple Martingale example in crash game:
- Round 1: bet 1,000, target 2x, loss
- Round 2: bet 2,000, target 2x, loss
- Round 3: bet 4,000, target 2x, loss
- Round 4: bet 8,000, target 2x, win → payout 16,000, total wagered 15,000, net profit 1,000

It looks reasonable on paper. Problems appear in the real world:

### Main Risks of Martingale

**Bankroll limits.** The sequence 1,000 → 2,000 → 4,000 → 8,000 → 16,000 → 32,000 only needs 6 consecutive losses. In crash game, 6 losses at a 2x target is not rare.

**Maximum bet limits.** Platforms cap the maximum bet per round. When Martingale bets hit the cap, the system breaks completely.

**Crash before target.** Martingale in crash game assumes you always cash out at your target. One crash faster than planned = the entire sequence is wiped out.

**Psychological pressure.** Large bets after a losing streak increase stress and push impulsive decisions.

Practical conclusion: Martingale is not a sustainable crash game strategy. If you want a progressive betting system, read the bankroll principles in [crypto bankroll management](/blog/manajemen-bankroll-crypto) first and set a strict daily loss limit.
