**Dice** is one of the easiest games to understand: pick roll under or roll over, then adjust your win chance and payout target. It is a great entry point for players who want to learn probability before moving to higher-variance games.

## Core Dice Mechanics

- **Roll Under**: you win when the result is below your selected threshold.
- **Roll Over**: you win when the result is above your selected threshold.
- Higher multipliers always come with lower win probability.

## Understand the House Edge

Every wager carries a small house edge. There is no guaranteed winning setup, so long-term results depend on variance control and discipline rather than "secret systems."

## Strategy 1: High Win Chance (70-85%)

Use a high win chance for smoother balance movement. You will win more often, but each win pays less.

**Pros:** easier emotional control, beginner-friendly rhythm.  
**Cons:** one oversized loss can erase many small wins if stake size is not controlled.

## Strategy 2: Balanced Multipliers (2x-3x)

Target a medium range where win rate and payout are more balanced. A simple flat bet around 1% of bankroll works better than emotional bet sizing.

## Strategy 3: High Risk Sessions

10x+ multipliers should be treated as a small, predefined risk budget, not a default session style.

> Never run unlimited Martingale without strict stop-loss limits.

## Practice Plan

1. Open **Games -> Dice**.
2. Test 50%, 70%, and 90% win chance settings.
3. Play 50 rounds with minimum bet and compare variance.

## Final Takeaway

The best beginner Dice strategy is simple: respect probability, keep stake size small, and prioritize consistency over short-term adrenaline.
