## How Plinko Works: Ball, Pegs, and Slots

Strategy only helps after you understand the board.

### The Ball

Each bet releases one ball from the **top center** of the board. The ball falls vertically; at each peg it bounces **left or right** with roughly 50/50 probability on a standard symmetric board. After passing all peg rows, it lands in one **multiplier slot** on the bottom row.

On HokiMainbet, ball animation represents a path already determined by server seed, client seed, and nonce — not real-time physics the house could steer mid-drop.

### Pegs

Pegs form a triangular grid. Row 1 has one peg, row 2 has two, and so on. More rows mean more left/right decisions and a wider spread of outcomes across bottom slots.

Standard crypto Plinko follows a **binomial distribution**: balls cluster toward center slots (highest probability) and rarely reach edge slots (extreme multipliers).

### Multiplier Slots

Each bottom slot has a multiplier — for example 0.2x, 0.5x, 1x, 2x, 10x, 100x, or higher on aggressive boards. Slot values change with **risk level** (Low, Medium, High). Center slots usually offer modest multipliers; edge slots offer extremes — very high or very low.

Total payout equals bet size times the multiplier of the slot where the ball lands.

## Risk Levels: Low, Medium, and High

Besides row count, **risk level** is the most important setting. It changes the multiplier layout on slots — not the ball path, which remains Provably Fair.

### Low Risk

- Slot multipliers stay **conservative** — many slots around 0.8x to 1.5x; extreme 100x+ slots are rare or absent.
- **Low variance** — small wins happen often; brutal losses are uncommon.
- **Best for:** beginners, long sessions, small bankrolls, players who dislike emotional roller coasters.

Example Low Risk profile at 16 rows: center slots often land near 1x to 1.2x; edge slots might cap around 5x to 10x. You rarely wipe out in one drop, but you also rarely hit a spectacular payout.

### Medium Risk

- Balance between safe center slots and extreme edges.
- **Moderate variance** — chase multipliers in the 50x to 200x range without the brutality of High.
- **Best for:** intermediate players who know Plinko basics and want entertainment plus upside.

Medium is the default choice for players bored by Low but not mentally ready for High.

### High Risk

- Edge slots can reach **very high multipliers** (500x, 1000x, or platform maximum).
- Center slots may include harsh lows (0.2x, 0.3x) — frequent small losses fund edge potential.
- **High variance** — long dry spells possible; one edge hit can recover many losses.
- **Best for:** experienced players, dedicated entertainment budget, those who treat Plinko as lottery-style upside.

High Risk is not "harder to win" in a rigged sense — the math is published. It is **harder to endure psychologically**.

## Row Count: 8, 12, and 16

Row count changes how many peg decisions the ball makes before landing.

### 8 Rows

- **Fastest rounds** — quick feedback, good for testing settings.
- **Narrower distribution** — fewer extreme edge outcomes relative to 16 rows.
- **Best for:** first sessions, auto-drop experiments, players who want many drops per minute.

### 12 Rows

- Middle ground between speed and spread.
- Slightly more edge hits than 8 rows at the same risk level.
- **Best for:** players transitioning from Low 8 rows toward more volatility without jumping straight to 16.

### 16 Rows

- **Maximum spread** on standard boards — edge slots are hardest to hit but most rewarding.
- Longer animation per drop; fewer drops per minute than 8 rows.
- **Best for:** players who chose Medium or High risk intentionally and accept variance.

Rule of thumb: **more rows amplify whatever risk level you selected**. High risk at 16 rows is the most volatile common setup.

## Strategies by Player Type

Plinko is not beatable long-term through pattern prediction — paths are hashed before the drop. Strategy means **bankroll and setting discipline**, not guessing left vs right.

### Conservative Player (Low Risk, 8–12 Rows)

- Bet **1% or less** of demo or real bankroll per drop.
- Use **8 or 12 rows** for steadier session curves.
- Set a **stop-win and stop-loss** (for example +15% / -10% of session bankroll) and leave when hit.
- Avoid chasing edge slots — that is High Risk behavior, not Low.

This foundation works well for everyone learning on [Plinko demo](/games/plinko).

### Balanced Player (Medium Risk, 12 Rows)

- Flat bet size for at least 100 drops before changing stake.
- Track average multiplier per 50 drops in a spreadsheet or notes app.
- If variance feels too wild, drop to Low at the same row count instead of increasing bet size.

Medium at 12 rows is the "default serious" profile for many Indonesian crypto players in 2026.

### Aggressive Player (High Risk, 16 Rows)

- Treat bankroll as **entertainment budget** — money you can lose entirely.
- Use **smaller bet fractions** (0.5% per drop) because variance is extreme.
- Never increase bet size after a near-miss on the edge — that is gambler's fallacy.
- Pre-define a maximum number of auto drops (see below).

### Session Structure That Works

1. **Warm up in demo** — 50 drops at your intended risk and row settings.
2. **Record** highest and lowest multipliers — feel the variance before real stakes.
3. **Single setting per session** — do not switch Low to High mid-loss; that mixes profiles and confuses decisions.
4. **End on time**, not on emotion — timer beats tilt.

## Auto Drop: Speed, Limits, and Pitfalls

Auto drop releases many balls without manual clicks — essential for volume, dangerous without limits.

### Smart Auto Drop Settings

- **Number of balls:** cap at 50–200 per session segment; reset and reassess between segments.
- **Bet size:** fixed only — no "increase on loss" chains.
- **Same risk and rows:** changing mid-auto defeats planning.

### Common Auto Drop Mistakes

- Running 1000 High Risk 16-row drops on a small bankroll — variance will eat it.
- Staring at animation hypnotized while balance bleeds — use sound-off and check balance every N drops.
- Believing "due for edge hit" after many center lands — each drop is independent.

Auto drop is a tool for **consistent execution**, not a way to force a 1000x.
