## Keno Basics: Pick, Draw, Match

Every keno round follows three phases:

### Pick (Choose Numbers)

The keno board usually displays numbers **1–80**. You select **1 to 10 numbers** (limits vary by platform — HokiMainbet follows standard range). Your chosen numbers are called **picks**.

Pick strategy is the main player decision — how many numbers, which numbers, quick pick vs manual selection.

### Draw (Number Call)

The platform randomly draws **20 numbers** from 80. Visually, numbers appear one by one or all at once. These 20 numbers are the **draw** or **called numbers**.

You do not influence the draw — only your picks matter.

### Match (Compare)

**Match** equals how many of your picks also appear in the draw. More matches generally mean higher payout — but the relationship is not linear; the payout table defines exact multipliers.

Example: Pick 5 numbers. Draw produces 3 of your numbers → payout follows the "5 pick, 3 match" row in the table.

### House Edge in Keno

Keno generally carries house edge of **5–25%** depending on picks and table — higher than blackjack, comparable to lottery. Keno is not a "skill edge" game — strategy focuses on **variance management** and **entertainment value**, not guaranteed long-term profit.

## Few Numbers vs Many Numbers Strategy

Classic keno debate: pick **few** (1–4) vs **many** (8–10)?

### Few Numbers Strategy (1–4 Picks)

**Advantages:**
- Full match more achievable
- Payout per full match moderate but realistic
- Moderate variance — losing streaks shorter than 10-pick

**Example:** 3 picks, need 3 match for big payout. Probability of 3/3 is higher than 10/10.

**Best for:** Beginners, long sessions, small bankroll.

### Many Numbers Strategy (8–10 Picks)

**Advantages:**
- Partial match (5/10, 6/10) still pays
- Extreme jackpot potential on full match
- Zero-match payout may apply

**Disadvantages:**
- Full match approaches lottery odds
- Many draws "almost" hit without significant payout
- Brutal variance — bankroll drains fast without discipline

**Best for:** Players who understand odds, large bankroll, limited lottery-style sessions.

## Basic Keno Odds

Keno uses **combinatorics** — hypergeometric distribution. Players do not need manual calculation every draw, but understanding the concept helps set realistic expectations.

### Match Probability (Intuition)

From 80 numbers, 20 are drawn. If you pick 5 numbers:
- Probability of **exactly 3 match** — moderate, happens fairly often
- Probability of **exactly 5 match** — rare, big payout
- Probability of **0 match** — possible, usually no payout for 5 picks

More picks make **partial match** easier — but full match becomes exponentially harder.

### Expected Value (EV)

EV = sum of (outcome probability × payout) minus bet

Keno EV is negative (house edge). The "best" strategy means **minimize bet relative to bankroll** and **maximize entertainment per unit lost** — not finding magic numbers.

### Keno Number Myths

- **"Hot numbers"** — frequently appearing recently → not predictive, draws are independent
- **"Cold numbers due"** — rarely appearing → gambler's fallacy
- **"Diagonal pattern always works"** → RNG does not see board layout

Quick pick vs manual: **identical odds** long term. Choose based on preference, not superstition.
