## What Is Crypto Dice Game?

Crypto Dice Game is a game where the system generates a random number (usually 0.00–99.99), and you choose whether the next roll will land **under** or **over** a specific threshold. The threshold determines **win chance** and **payout multiplier** — the two are inversely related.

Higher win chance → lower payout. Lower win chance → higher payout. There are no shortcuts: house edge exists in every configuration.

## Roll Under vs Roll Over: Probability Foundations

Before discussing strategy, understand the two basic modes:

### Roll Under

You win if the roll lands **under** the threshold you set. Example: Roll Under 50 = numbers 0.00–49.99 win, 50.00–99.99 lose. Win chance ≈ 50%, payout ≈ 1.98x (after house edge).

### Roll Over

You win if the roll lands **over** the threshold. Example: Roll Over 50 = numbers 50.01–99.99 win. Win chance and payout are mathematically identical to Roll Under 50 — only the threshold direction differs.

### Win Chance and Payout: The Mathematical Relationship

Basic formula (before house edge):

```
Payout ≈ 100 / Win Chance (%)
```

| Win Chance | Payout (theoretical) | Payout (with ~1% edge) |
|------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 90% | 1.11x | ~1.10x |
| 75% | 1.33x | ~1.32x |
| 50% | 2.00x | ~1.98x |
| 25% | 4.00x | ~3.96x |
| 10% | 10.00x | ~9.90x |
| 5% | 20.00x | ~19.80x |
| 1% | 100.00x | ~99.00x |

Understanding this table is the key to any **dice strategy**. Every strategy ultimately comes down to one decision: where you position yourself on the win chance vs payout spectrum.

## Low Risk Strategy: High Win Chance

**Low Risk strategy** in dice game means win chance of **70–95%** with low payout (1.05x–1.40x). This approach is popular among players who prioritize **volume and consistency** over large multipliers.

### Low Risk Characteristics

**High win rate.** You win 7–9 out of 10 rolls — it feels "easy" and suits beginners learning the mechanics.

**Small profit per win.** A 1.10x payout means net profit is only 10% of the bet. One loss requires 1 win to recover (not counting house edge).

**Low variance.** Sessions feel stable. Bankroll does not swing drastically over 50 rolls.

### Recommended Low Risk Setup

- Win chance: **85–90%**
- Payout: **~1.10x–1.15x**
- Bet: **1% bankroll** per roll
- Session target: 100 rolls with stop loss at -10% bankroll

Example with 100,000 demo coin bankroll:
- Bet per roll: 1,000 coins
- Win → +100–150 coin profit
- Loss → -1,000 coins
- Need ~7–10 wins to recover 1 loss

Low Risk feels safe, but **house edge erodes profit** at high volume. It is good for practicing discipline and understanding distribution — not for "winning big."

## High Risk Strategy: Low Win Chance

**High Risk strategy** means win chance of **1–15%** with payout of **7x–99x**. One win can replace dozens of losses — but dozens of losses can come before a single win.

### High Risk Characteristics

**Very low win rate.** Win chance of 5% = average 1 win per 20 rolls. A streak of 40+ losses before a win is normal statistically.

**Large payout per win.** One hit at 20x payout with a 1,000 coin bet = +19,000 coin profit.

**Extreme variance.** Bankroll can drop 30–50% before a single win recovers it. Requires a large bankroll and strong mental discipline.

### Recommended High Risk Setup

- Win chance: **5–10%**
- Payout: **~10x–19x**
- Bet: **0.5% bankroll** per roll (smaller than Low Risk!)
- Session stop loss: **-15% bankroll** (strict)

High Risk is **not** for beginners. If you are still learning **how to play dice**, start with Low/Medium risk first. High Risk without experience leads to fast tilt.

### High Risk vs Lottery

Win chance of 1% (100x payout) feels like a lottery — and it almost is. This strategy is for entertainment with bankroll you are prepared to lose, not for income. Limit High Risk sessions to 20–30 rolls maximum, then stop.

## Martingale in Dice Game: Deep Analysis

Martingale: **double your bet after every loss**, return to base bet after a win. Popular because it looks "mathematical" — but does it work in dice game?

### Martingale Example in Dice (Win Chance 49.5%, Payout 2x)

Base bet: 1,000 coins

| Roll | Bet | Result | Cumulative Profit/Loss |
|------|-----|--------|------------------------|
| 1 | 1,000 | Loss | -1,000 |
| 2 | 2,000 | Loss | -3,000 |
| 3 | 4,000 | Loss | -7,000 |
| 4 | 8,000 | Win (+8,000) | +1,000 |
| 5 | 1,000 | Loss | 0 |
| 6 | 2,000 | Win (+4,000) | +3,000 |

One win on roll 4 covers 3 previous losses + 1,000 profit. It looks effective.

### Problems with Martingale in Dice Game

**Long loss streaks.** Win chance 49.5% = probability of 6 consecutive losses ≈ 1.6%. Over 500 rolls, you **will** see 6+ loss streaks. Martingale bet on roll 6 = 64,000 coins from a 1,000 base.

**Accumulative house edge.** Every roll has ~1% edge. High-volume Martingale = edge erodes faster.

**Platform limits.** Max bet limits break Martingale sequences — when you cannot double, the strategy collapses.

**Tilt trigger.** A 32,000 coin bet to recover from a 1,000 base triggers extreme stress.

### Martingale Verdict

Martingale in **crypto dice game** can profit short-term, but has **negative expected value** long-term. Use only in demo, with very small base bets, max 4–5 doubling levels, and absolute stop loss. Never Martingale at High Risk — a fatal combination.

## Paroli (Reverse Martingale): Positive Progression Strategy

Paroli is the reverse of Martingale: **double your bet after a win**, reset to base after a loss. The idea: capitalize on win streaks with limited risk.

### Paroli Example in Dice (Win Chance 49.5%)

Base bet: 1,000 coins, max 3 levels

| Roll | Bet | Result | Session Profit |
|------|-----|--------|----------------|
| 1 | 1,000 | Win | +1,000 |
| 2 | 2,000 | Win | +3,000 |
| 3 | 4,000 | Loss | -1,000 |
| 4 | 1,000 | Win | 0 |
| 5 | 2,000 | Win | +2,000 |
| 6 | 4,000 | Win | +6,000 |
| 7 | 1,000 | (reset, take profit) | +6,000 |

Paroli limits exposure: worst case per cycle = loss of base bet (1,000), not the entire Martingale sequence.

### Paroli Advantages vs Martingale

**Limited risk per cycle.** You only risk the base bet when starting a new cycle.

**Positive psychology.** Win streaks feel rewarding; a loss only resets, it does not trigger a chase.

**Works with Low/Medium Risk.** Higher win rate = more frequent doubling triggers.

### Paroli Disadvantages

**Small profit in loss cycles.** One loss at level 3 = only level 1–2 profit lost, but mental effort remains.

**False streak confidence.** 3 consecutive wins at dice (49.5%) is not a "hot hand" — every roll is independent. Paroli works as money management, not prediction.

### Recommended Paroli Setup

- Base bet: 1% bankroll
- Max levels: 3 (1x → 2x → 4x → reset)
- Win chance: 70–90% (Low Risk) for higher cycle frequency
- Take profit: +5% bankroll per session, then stop

## Flat Betting: The Most Underrated Strategy

**Flat betting** = same bet every roll, with no progression. Not as exciting as Martingale or Paroli, but **the most honest expected value** and most predictable variance.

### Why Flat Bet Is the Best Dice Strategy for Beginners

**No exponential bet sequences.** Losses do not trigger 32x bet increases.

**Clean session data.** 100 rolls flat bet = pure probability data for evaluating win chance choice.

**Transparent house edge.** Profit/loss is a direct function of win rate vs payout, without progression distortion.

Setup:
- Win chance: 49.5%, payout ~2x
- Bet: 1,000 coins flat, 200 rolls
- Expectation: slight loss due to house edge (~1% of total volume wagered)

Flat bet 200 rolls in demo = **benchmark** before trying any progressive strategy.

## Common Dice Strategy Mistakes

### Win Chance Too Low Without Bankroll
Win chance 2% with 10,000 bankroll and 1,000 bet = maximum 10 rolls before bust. Match win chance to bankroll size.

### Changing Win Chance Mid-Session
Loss streak at 50% → switch to 90% "for recovery" → loss streak at 90% → tilt. Set your configuration, follow it for the entire session.

### Ignoring House Edge
"Payout 2x, win 50%, should break even." Edge ~1% means long-term break-even is impossible. Accept it; optimize for entertainment and discipline.

### Copying Streamer Strategy
Streamers play High Risk at 1% for content — different bankroll and goals. Their strategy is not your strategy.

## Dice Game Strategy FAQ

### Best dice strategy for beginners?

**Flat bet, win chance 49.5–90%, bet 1% bankroll.** Low Risk (85%+) for comfort, Medium (50%) for understanding variance.

### Can you win consistently at dice?

House edge guarantees negative EV long-term. "Consistent" means consistent discipline, not consistent profit. Demo allows practice without financial consequences.

### Martingale or Paroli?

Paroli is safer (limited loss per cycle). Martingale has higher bust risk. Both lose to house edge long-term. Flat bet is most honest for learning.

### Roll Under or Roll Over — is there a difference?

Mathematically identical. Choose based on UI preference or superstition — not strategic advantage.

### Minimum rolls for strategy evaluation?

Minimum **200 rolls** per setting for meaningful data. 50 rolls = statistical noise.
