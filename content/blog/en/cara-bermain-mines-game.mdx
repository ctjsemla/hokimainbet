Every tile you reveal increases the multiplier; every mine you hit destroys your bet. There is no rising chart like Crash, no instant roll like Dice — Mines is a game of **sequential decisions** where greed and psychology become your biggest enemies. ## What Is Mines Game?

Mines Game displays a tile grid (usually 5×5 = 25 tiles) with a number of **hidden mines** that you choose before the round starts. You reveal tiles one at a time; each safe tile increases the payout multiplier. You can **cash out** at any time after at least one safe tile is revealed — or keep opening tiles for a higher multiplier with the risk of hitting a mine.

Simple example:
- 5×5 grid, 3 mines, 10,000 coin bet
- Open 1 safe tile → multiplier ~1.08x, can cash out ~10,800
- Open 5 safe tiles → multiplier ~1.55x, can cash out ~15,500
- Open a mine tile → 10,000 coin bet lost

Mine positions are determined before you click the first tile — via a cryptographic system similar to [Provably Fair](/blog/apa-itu-provably-fair). There are no "lucky tiles" based on click timing.

## How to Play Mines Game: Step by Step

### Step 1: Choose Mine Count

Before betting, decide how many mines are on the grid. Common range: **1–24 mines** on a 25-tile grid. More mines = multiplier rises faster per safe tile, but probability of hitting a mine on each click is also higher.

### Step 2: Set Your Bet

Decide bet size according to your bankroll. Rule of thumb: **1–2% bankroll per round** — same as other games. Full bankroll rules are in [crypto bankroll management](/blog/manajemen-bankroll-crypto).

### Step 3: Open Tiles

Click tiles one at a time. Each safe tile shows a new multiplier and cash out option. A mine tile = round over, bet lost.

### Step 4: Cash Out or Continue

After every safe tile, you face a decision: **take profit now** or **open the next tile**. This is the heart of mines strategy — not guessing mine positions, but managing sequential risk.

## Conservative Strategy: Open Few, Cash Out Early

**Conservative mines strategy** prioritizes **survival rate** — cash out after 2–4 safe tiles with low mine count (1–5).

### Recommended Conservative Setup

- Mines: **3** (of 25 tiles)
- Target opens: **3 tiles**, then cash out
- Bet: **1% bankroll**
- Expected multiplier: ~1.30x–1.45x per successful round

### Why Conservative Works for Beginners

**High win rate.** With 3 mines and stopping at 3 tiles, success probability:

```
P(safe 3 clicks) = (22/25) × (21/24) × (20/23) ≈ 67%
```

About 2 in 3 rounds succeed — achievable and builds confidence.

**Greed minimized.** Fixed target of 3 tiles = no "just one more tile" syndrome.

**Bankroll preservation.** Loss is only 1% per failed round; win adds +30–45% of base bet per success.

### Conservative Variation: 1 Mine Grind

1 mine on 25 tiles = very safe per-click probability. Open 5–8 tiles, cash out ~1.20x–1.40x. High win rate, small profit per round — similar to Low Risk in [best dice strategies](/blog/strategi-dice-terbaik). Good for long discipline practice sessions.

## Aggressive Strategy: Multiplier Hunt

**Aggressive mines strategy** targets **8–15+ tiles** with medium-high mines (8–15). One successful round = big payout; many rounds fail on tile 3 or 5.

### Aggressive Setup

- Mines: **10**
- Target opens: **8 tiles**, cash out
- Bet: **0.5% bankroll** (smaller than conservative!)
- Expected multiplier if successful: ~10x–20x

### Aggressive Reality Check

```
P(safe 8 clicks, 10 mines) = (15/25) × (14/24) × ... × (8/17) ≈ 3–5%
```

Only 3–5% of rounds reach the target — meaning 95%+ rounds fail. Requires mental preparedness and bankroll that can absorb 20+ round loss streaks.

### When Aggressive Makes Sense

- Dedicated fun money bankroll
- After mastering conservative play for 100+ rounds
- Limited sessions (max 20 rounds), strict stop loss
- **Not** a main strategy for beginners

## Expected Value: The Math Behind Mines Game

**Expected value (EV)** is average profit/loss per round if repeated infinitely. In casino games, EV is usually negative (house edge). Understanding mines EV prevents the illusion of a "guaranteed winning strategy."

### Simple EV Concept

```
EV = (P(win) × Profit) - (P(loss) × Bet)
```

Example: 3 mines, target 3 tiles, 1,000 bet, success multiplier ~1.35x

```
P(win) ≈ 0.67
P(loss) ≈ 0.33
Profit if win = 350
EV = (0.67 × 350) - (0.33 × 1000) = 234.5 - 330 = -95.5
```

Negative EV — house edge. **No tile count or mine count produces positive EV** long-term. Mines strategy is about **variance management**, not beating math.

### Practical Implications

- Conservative: small negative EV per round, low variance
- Aggressive: similar negative EV, very high variance
- Choose strategy based on **risk tolerance**, not fake positive EV

## Tile Opening Patterns: Myth vs Reality

Many mines players believe **tile opening patterns** affect outcomes — corners first, center first, zigzag, spiral. This needs a clear clarification.

### Mine Positions Are Already Fixed

Before your first click, the system assigns mines to grid positions. Your click order **does not affect** mine positions. Opening corners vs center only changes **reveal order**, not overall outcome probability.

### Patterns as Discipline Tools

Even though patterns do not change math, a **fixed pattern** is useful for psychological discipline:

**Corner-In Pattern:** Click 4 corners first → if safe, 1 center tile → cash out. Provides decision structure, prevents random clicking.

**Row Scan Pattern:** Click top row left-to-right, stop after N safe tiles. Predictable, easy to repeat.

**Random Pattern (Anti-Pattern):** Deliberately random to avoid superstition attachment. Some players prefer this for mental freedom.

Use patterns as **discipline rituals**, not mine prediction.

### "Feeling" a Tile

There is none. Every unopened tile has equal probability of containing a mine (conditional on remaining mines/tiles). Clicking "the one that feels safe" is gambler's fallacy.

## Mines Game Psychology: The Player's Biggest Enemy

Mines is more psychological than Crash or Dice because **you actively choose to continue** after every safe tile. The multiplier rises in front of you; the cash out button is always visible; greed whispers "just one more."

### Greedy Continuation Trap

Classic scenario:
1. Target: 3 tiles, cash out
2. Tile 1 safe → "ok"
3. Tile 2 safe → "ok"
4. Tile 3 safe → multiplier 1.35x → "a bit more, 1.35x is small"
5. Tile 4 mine → total loss

Step 4 is where strategy dies. **Pre-commitment target** before the round — write it, follow it consistently.

### Loss Recovery Urge

Lose 3 rounds in a row → raise bet 3x "to recover." Classic chasing losses. Mines loss streaks are normal at any mine count — doubling bet does not change mine positions.

### Near-Miss Effect

Open 7 safe tiles of an 8-tile target, hit mine on tile 8. Brain registers "almost won" and urges immediate retry. **Near-miss = full loss.** Treat it as such.

### Antidotes

- **Hard tile count target** — no adjustment mid-round
- **Session stop loss** — -10% bankroll = close game
- **Break after 3 consecutive losses** — 5 minutes away
- **Demo practice** — build habit without financial sting

## Common Mines Game Beginner Mistakes

### Too Many Mines Too Soon
15 mines looks exciting. First click has 60% survival. Second ~55%. Most beginners bust in rounds 1–3 repeatedly then get frustrated. Start with 3 mines.

### No Pre-Commitment Target
"Play first, decide later." = always push one more tile. Always lose to greed. Write target before betting.

### Raising Bets After Winning
Big multiplier → euphoria → next bet 5x → hit mine immediately. Flat bet regardless of previous result.

### Ignoring Bankroll Rules
Mines rounds are fast (10–30 seconds). 100 rounds per hour is possible. 1% × 100 failed rounds in theory — in practice variance is harsher. Session limits are mandatory.

### Believing Hot/Cold Grids
"Lots of mines on the left last round, right side is safe now." Independent events. Random positions every round.

## Mines Game FAQ

### Best mine count for beginners?

**3 mines** on a 5×5 grid. Balance between meaningful multiplier growth and safe click probability.

### How many tiles to open before cash out?

**2–4 tiles** for conservative play (3 mines). Set the number before starting; use auto cashout if available.

### Do tile opening patterns help?

They do not change probability. They only help discipline and decision consistency.

### Mines vs Crash — which is easier?

Crash: one decision (when to cash out). Mines: many decisions (continue or stop each tile). Crash is simpler for beginners; Mines trains sequential discipline.

### Can you profit consistently at mines?

No — house edge guarantees negative EV. **Consistent discipline** is possible; **consistent profit** is not realistic long-term.
